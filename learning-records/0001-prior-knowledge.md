# Prior knowledge established (session 1)

**Status:** active

The learner can already:
- Play open chords (e.g. G, C, D, Em, Am, E, A) — claimed comfort, depth
  unverified.
- Play several contemporary worship songs from memory.

The learner explicitly does **not** know:
- Any music theory.
- Any scales.

## Implications

This sets a firm floor and ceiling for the zone of proximal development:

- **Skip** beginner-chord and strumming material. Don't re-teach open chords.
- **Start at module 0 (fretboard landmarks)** — naming notes on the neck —
  because chord-shape fluency without note-name fluency is exactly the gap
  that blocks everything downstream (scales, diatonic chords, transposition).
- **Theory is taught from scratch** but should always be tied back to shapes
  the learner's hands already know (e.g. "the G chord you already play *is*
  scale degrees 1-3-5 of G major").
- **Ear training has no prior floor** — start functional/scale-degree drills
  from the very beginning alongside the theory, not after.

## Evidence

Stated directly by the learner in the initial session ("I already know how to
play chords and I can learn some songs by memory, but I don't know the music
theory or any scale"). Depth of chord knowledge not yet tested — verify
informally in early lessons and update this record if claims don't match
reality.
