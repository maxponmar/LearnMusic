Interval = number of frets. A major 3rd is always 4 frets, anywhere on the neck. A perfect 5th is always 7 frets. Intervals are moveable; they don't care what note you start from.

The major scale recipe

From any starting note, walk:

W — W — H — W — W — W — H

Whole = 2 frets, Half = 1 fret. The two half-steps always fall between scale degrees 3–4 and 7–1. That placement is the entire sound of "major."

Scale degrees & their personalities

#namesound / job
1tonicHome. Total rest.
2supertonicRestless; wants to resolve.
3mediantBright, stable. Defines "major."
4subdominantSuspended; wants to fall to 3.
5dominantStrong; second home.
6submediantWistful (relative minor's home).
7leading toneTense; yearns up to 1.
Learn in this order: 1, 3, 5 (the structural triad) → then 4 (suspended) → then 7 (tense) → then 2 and 6. Practice with the ear trainer 5–10 min daily.
