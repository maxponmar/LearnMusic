Rule of thumb: capo fret = song key − G's fret. For C, capo 5. For A, capo 2. For Bb, capo 3. For E, capo 9. That's the whole math.

Sunday-morning checklist

  1. Find the song's key by ear (or chart).
  2. Ask the singer what key they want.
  3. Capo to a position that uses the shapes you know.
  4. Sanity-check: is the CAGED fill region comfortable?
  5. Plan the dynamic arc before you start. Soft → build → full → soft.
  6. Lead with space. Let the vocal carry the song. Fill only where the song needs a voice.

The two taste habits (re-stated)

  1. Leave space. The shortest valid worship fill is one note.
  2. Repeat before you vary. A motif repeated is a melody. A motif varied once has weight.

The "one well-placed note" rule

When in doubt: play less. A single chord tone, struck once on beat 1 of a chord change, is a fill. The shortest valid worship fill is one note.
