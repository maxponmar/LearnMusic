# Mission: Learn Music & Acoustic Guitar (with purpose)

## Why
Play music **with understanding** — not just songs memorized finger-for-finger. The goal is
musicianship: rhythm, scales, keys, and ears that let you learn new songs and improvise with
confidence.

## Success looks like
- Keep steady time with a metronome while strumming or picking.
- Name notes on the fretboard and hear them as scale degrees (1–7).
- Understand why chords in a key fit together — and hear common progressions by ear.
- Play song sections **in time** with guitar + click, not just from muscle memory.
- Optional stretch: tasteful fills using pentatonic shapes.

## Constraints
- **Instrument (v1):** acoustic guitar fully supported; ukulele, banjo, piano stubbed for later.
- **Starting point:** knows open chords; can play songs from memory; little or no theory/scales.
- **Single learner.** No auth; structured so others can fork the repo.
- **General theory first.** Worship (and other) song charts live in the Library as repertoire tracks.

## Out of scope (v1)
- Sight reading standard notation.
- Mic / pitch detection for grading (app-plays ear training only).
- Full multi-instrument UI (beyond guitar fretboard).
- Jazz harmony depth, band leading, electric tone.

## The path (7 modules)
0. **Time & rhythm** — metronome, quarter notes, strumming in time.
1. **Your instrument** — open strings, string names, play with the click.
2. **Notes & major scale** — landmarks on the neck, G major, intervals.
3. **Scale degrees** — hear 1-3-5, find them on the neck.
4. **Chords & keys** — triads, Nashville numbers, I-IV-V.
5. **Play with purpose** — progressions by ear, song sections with metronome.
6. **Improv basics** — pentatonic, motifs, space (optional stretch).

Modules 0–1 are rhythm and instrument. Module 5 is the first big payoff: playing with purpose
instead of memorization only.

## Repertoire
Worship and general song charts are tagged in the Library (`worship`, `general`, etc.). They are
**applied practice**, not the core curriculum frame.
