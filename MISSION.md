# Mission: Learn Worship Guitar (by ear, not by memory)

## Why
Play worship songs for God **without depending on memorization** — to hear a
song and be able to play it, and to improvise tastefully under the vocal.
Knowing chords and song-forms from memory is a gift, but it caps you at the
songs you've already learned. The goal here is the underlying musicianship:
theory, ear, and improv that let you serve *any* song on the spot.

## Success looks like
- Hear a contemporary worship chorus (Hillsong/Bethel/Elevation/Tomlin
  territory) → identify the key and the chord numbers → play it.
- Improvise fills over a I-V-vi-IV loop using major and minor pentatonic,
  serving the vocal instead of competing with it.
- Read a Nashville Number System chart and transpose it to any key on the fly
  (with capo strategy when needed).
- Pick out a melody by ear using scale-degree recognition (functional ear).

## Constraints
- **Instrument:** acoustic guitar only.
- **Starting point:** knows open chords; can play songs from memory; zero
  music theory and no scales. (Recorded in `learning-records/0001`.)
- **Single learner.** The app is built for one user; no auth, no multi-tenant
  complexity.
- **Repertoire focus:** contemporary worship. Keys G/D/C/A, heavy capo use,
  I-V-vi-IV and sus/add9 vocabulary.

## Out of scope (v1)
- Sight reading standard notation.
- Jazz harmony (extended/altered chords, tritone subs).
- Modes beyond Mixolydian-as-color.
- Electric-guitar tone design and pedal chains.
- Leading a band / running rehearsals.

These are good things — just not now. They protect the zone of proximal
development so we don't sprawl into "all of guitar."

## The path (6 modules)
0. **Fretboard landmarks** — name any note on the neck.
1. **Intervals & the major scale** — hear a note as a scale degree (1–7).
2. **Diatonic chords + Nashville Number System** — why G-C-Em-D always works.
3. **Progressions by ear** — hear a chorus → play the numbers in any key.
4. **Pentatonic + CAGED** — play tasteful fills over a loop.
5. **Improvisation serving the song** — motifs, space, capo strategy.

Modules 0–2 are foundation. Module 3 is the first mission-critical payoff
(play songs by ear). Modules 4–5 deliver the improv goal.
