# Worship Guitar Resources

Curated, high-trust sources for this curriculum. Knowledge is drawn from the
"Knowledge" section; wisdom (real-world feedback) from "Communities." Each
entry is annotated with what it covers and when to reach for it. Source tiers:
**A** = recognized authority, **B** = credible practitioner, **C** = community.

## Knowledge — music theory (instrument-neutral)

- **[Book] Tom Kolb — *Music Theory for Guitarists* (Hal Leonard)** [A]
  The standard first theory book for guitarists. Intervals → scales → chords
  → modes → ear training, with audio. Use for: foundational reference. The
  safest starting point for an adult learner.
  https://www.halleonard.com/product/695730/music-theory-for-guitarists

- **musictheory.net — Lessons** [A]
  Free, sequential, peer-cited. The relevant chain: Lesson 40 (major scale) →
  41 (scale degrees) → 43 (diatonic triads) → 44 (Roman numerals).
  Use for: the authoritative backbone of theory concepts (modules 1–2).
  https://www.musictheory.net/lessons

- **Open Music Theory (VIVA Pressbooks, peer-reviewed OER)** [A]
  University-level. Roman-numeral chapter for rigorous definitions.
  https://viva.pressbooks.pub/openmusictheory/chapter/roman-numerals/

- **University of Puget Sound — "Diatonic Chords in Major"** [A]
  Clean academic statement of the I-ii-iii-IV-V-vi-vii° pattern.
  https://musictheory.pugetsound.edu/mt21c/DiatonicChordsInMajor.html

## Knowledge — guitar-specific theory & fretboard

- **[Book] Desi Serna — *Fretboard Theory* Vol. I** [A/B]
  The bridge from "theory" to "fretboard application." CAGED, pentatonic &
  major scales, modes, progressions, applied to real songs. Prereq: open &
  barre chords (matches the learner's starting point). Use for: modules 2–4.
  https://www.guitarmusictheory.com/

- **JustinGuitar — Practical Music Theory course** [A]
  Free structured course (Grades/Modules): notes → intervals → major scale →
  keys → chords in a key → progressions. ~3M users. Use for: validating the
  curriculum sequence and as a parallel reference for the learner.
  https://www.justinguitar.com/classes/practical-music-theory-course-fast-hands-on

- **MusicTheoryForGuitar.com — Tommaso Zillio** [B]
  "What to learn (and in what order)" — defends sequencing as a pedagogical
  lever. Use for: defending the *order* of modules (intervals before scales,
  diatonic harmony before modes).
  https://www.musictheoryforguitar.com/learningmusictheoryintherightorder.html

## Knowledge — ear training

- **Functional Ear Trainer (Munal Music) — app** [B]
  Free iOS/Android/desktop. Plays a cadence to establish key → plays a note →
  you identify the scale degree. This *is* the movable-Do / scale-degree
  method, operationalized. Use for: **daily** ear-training practice (module 1+).
  https://munnal.com/

- **ToneSavvy — Functional (Solfege) Scale-Degree exercise** [B]
  Browser-based cadence-then-note drills. Use for: no-install alternative.
  https://tonesavvy.com/music-practice-exercise/220/functional-solfege-scale-degree-ear/

- **Jeff Schneider — "Functional vs Intervallic Ear Training"** [B]
  Defends functional (scale-degree) training as the better tool for tonal
  music. Use for: the rationale behind choosing functional-first.
  https://jeffschneidermusic.com/blog/functional-vs-intervallic-ear-training

## Knowledge — Nashville Number System (the worship band's lingua franca)

- **Lipscomb University — "The Nashville Number System: A Framework for
  Teaching Harmony" (JMTP, peer-reviewed)** [A]
  Academic underpinning of NNS as functional-harmony notation. Highest-trust
  source on NNS specifically. Use for: the rigorous foundation.
  https://digitalcollections.lipscomb.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1199&context=jmtp

- **MxU — "The Nashville Number System"** [B]
  Calls NNS "the Rosetta Stone of worship music." Use for: framing *why* NNS
  is the connective tissue between ear and instrument in a worship context.
  https://app.getmxu.com/lessons/the-nashville-number-system

- **Collaborate Worship — "Nashville Number System for Guitar Players"** [B]
  Clean diatonic-to-numbers mapping (1, 4, 5 major; 2m, 3m, 6m minor; 7° dim).
  https://collaborateworship.com/the-nashville-number-system-for-guitar-players-a-simple-guide/

## Knowledge — worship-music context & repertoire

- **CCLI Top 100 (SongSelect)** [A]
  The canonical list of what churches actually report singing. Use for:
  ground-truth song pool for ear-training material and examples.
  https://songselect.ccli.com/ccli-top-100

- **Worship Leader Research — "The CCLI Top 100 Changed, And Nobody Is Talking
  About It"** [B]
  Independent trend analysis (51 new songs 2020–2025). Use for: current
  repertoire awareness.
  https://worshipleaderresearch.com/the-ccli-top-100-changed-and-nobody-is-talking-about-it/

- **Musicademy — "Choosing the right key for small church worship"** [B]
  Explains the congregational-range principle and why songs get re-charted in
  G/D/C. Use for: the *why* behind key choice & capo strategy.
  https://www.musicademy.com/blog/right-key-small-church-worship/

## Knowledge — worship improvisation

- **Worship Tutorials (Brian Wahl) — "How To Learn Lead Parts In Songs Using
  Scales"** [B]
  Uses the major scale + minor pentatonic to find lead parts in worship songs.
  Use for: core scale vocabulary for fills (module 4).
  https://worshiptutorials.com/guitar-lessons/how-to-learn-lead-parts-in-songs-using-scales/

- **10 Week Worship Guitar — "Lead Guitar 5: From Scales to Music"** [B]
  The key insight: change pentatonic scale *per chord* rather than one scale
  over the whole progression. Use for: the "follow the chord" mindset.
  https://10weekworshipguitar.com/lead-guitar-5-from-scales-to-music/

- **Worship Artistry — "The Best Way to Improve at Lead Guitar"** [B]
  Stresses practicing scales in context over real worship progressions.
  Use for: methodology of contextual practice.
  https://worshipartistry.com/greenroom/musicianship/skill-building/the-best-way-to-improve-at-lead-guitar

## Gaps (honest notes)

- **No peer-reviewed "chords → improvise" curriculum exists.** The 6-module
  path is a synthesis of strong practitioner consensus, not a validated
  program. We cross-check across multiple B-tier sources to compensate.
- **Functional vs intervallic ear training lacks head-to-head data** for
  guitarists specifically. "Functional first" rests on jazz/theory educator
  consensus, not experiments.
- **"Play melodies by ear" is the weakest-served step** in mainstream books.
  It lives in the ear-training tools (Functional Ear Trainer, ToneSavvy), not
  in theory books. We pair the two.
- **Mixolydian as a "core worship scale" is overstated** in some YouTube
  content. Cross-checking sources, pentatonics + the major scale dominate.
  We treat Mixolydian as situational color, not foundational.
