The Nashville Number System — chords as numbers

The Nashville Number System (NNS) is how working worship musicians communicate chord progressions: as numbers, not letters. A 1-5-6-4 progression is the same song whether the band plays it in G or A or D. Learn it once and you can transpose anything on the fly.

Why this matters for your mission

Two reasons. First, your church's worship leader almost certainly calls songs in numbers when substituting keys. Second — and more important for playing by ear — most contemporary worship reduces to a handful of number patterns. If you can hear a chorus and think "that's 1-5-6-4," you can play it in any key instantly. Numbers are the universal chord language.

The NNS is "the Rosetta Stone of worship music" — the connective tissue between your ear, the chart, and the fretboard. (MxU)

The NNS, in 30 seconds

In a major key:

  • 1, 4, 5 are major chords.
  • 2, 3, 6 are minor (often written 2-, 3-, 6-).
  • 7 is diminished (7°) — rarely used in worship.

Same as Roman numerals (I-ii-iii-IV-V-vi-vii°), just with arabic numbers.

The payoff: same numbers, any key

Take the progression 1-5-6-4. In three different keys:

key156-4
GGDEmC
DDABmG
CCGAmF

Same song. Same emotional shape. Different concrete chords. This is why the band leader can say "let's do it in A instead" and everyone adjusts instantly — the numbers didn't change.

Transpose on the fly: if you know a song as 1-5-6-4 and the leader says "A," you instantly know: A (1), E (5), F#m (6-), D (4). No re-memorizing. The numbers did the work.

Capo strategy — making any key guitar-friendly

Here's where it gets practical. Recorded worship songs are often in singer's keys (Db, Eb, Bb) — terrible for open-position guitar. The worship-guitar solution is the capo: use chord shapes from a friendly key (G, D, C, A) and capo up to reach the actual key.

The shapes you play stay friendly (G, C, D, Em — all open); the capo transposes the pitch. You think in numbers + friendly shapes; the capo handles the actual key.

Your win for today (20 minutes)

  1. Pick a worship song you know by heart in G (e.g. one using G-D-Em-C). Identify each chord as a number: 1-5-6-4? 1-4-5?
  2. Now transpose those numbers to D. Play the song in D using the same numbers. (G→D, C→G, Em→Bm, D→A for a 1-4-6-5 pattern.)
  3. Pick a song you know in G and figure out where to put the capo to play it in Bb. (Answer: capo 3, using G shapes — G shapes + 3 semitones = Bb.)
